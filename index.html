  • RA and TA are stipend based posts. You don't get tuition waivers. PhD students get their tuition waived by grants and scholarships. In many colleges your RA comes with a grant or a fellowship or a hefty scholarship. That's why they say "he/she got his tuition waived because of being a RA". NYU doesn't offer that much tuition waiver to foreign master's students.
  • Now, about TA, you got like 15-20 subjects, so around 70ish TAs. Each faculty typically has a couple of RAs. So I guess 30ish (not counting industry profs). You can apply for RA in other colleges as well. These all are to subject of availability and compatibility Labs also are a separate source of RA jobs, but sometimes the lines blur.
  • Now the GA. GA are things like desk jobs or maintenance guys or things like NYU IT, student devs, wasserman crew etc.
  • Courant makes a lot of fuss about RA and TA, I don't know, but there is no rule that grad students can't RA/TA.
